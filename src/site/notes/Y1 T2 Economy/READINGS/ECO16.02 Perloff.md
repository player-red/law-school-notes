pp. 172-175
Firms and production

Types of firms
- Private (for-profit)
- Public
- Non-profit

Ownership of For-Profit Firms
- Sole proprietorship: owned by one individual
- Partnerships: owned and jointly controlled by two or more people operating under an agreement
- Corporations: owned by shareholders.
	- Owners have limited liability for the firm's debts.

Management of Firms
- Owners, managers, and supervisors are all decision-makers in larger firms.
- Conflicts between the owners and managers can exist.

What Owners Want
- Try to maximize profits.
	- Profit = Revenue — Cost
	- Revenue = Price x Quantity 
- Efficient production (technological efficiency) is a necessary condition for profit maximization.
