Introduction
- The Roman Empire ran on the labour of the slaves, who were captured, sold (in various ways: foreign slaves, self-slavery, child sale, debt bondage, etc.).
- Escaping was difficult, but the Empire often allowed manumission. 

Entry Into Slavery
- Captive taking and slaveholding was a common practice and it became part of the Roman legal system.
	- Divided the ius gentium and the ius civile (law of all people v civil law)
- Slave status depended only on the mother, ie an enslaved mother only gave birth to more enslaved children, and a free mother gave birth to free children. 
- The father's status did not matter.
- Massive captive-taking events were also another way of obtaining slvaes.
- Some slaves were craftsmen, and others were trained in service professions.
- Slave systems were used to integrate captured people into the system of their Empire.
- Some regions were preferred over others as sources of slaves.
- Some provincial subjects, not considered citizens (citizenship was not automatically given, it was only for local elites) were also enslaved.
- Child abandonment, even of Roman citizens, was another large source of the supply of slaves.
- In the earlier years of the empire (upto 326 BCE) it was possible for even citizens to fall into slavery (usually by debt bondage).
- After Christianity was adopted, many practices changed in order to keep Christian values.

Experiences of Enslavement — Labour Extraction
- Slaves were often used for the personal entertainment of the Roman citizens.
- The Roman economy was also incredibly complex and could compete with the GDP of early European economies.
- Slaves were present in almost all economic sectors, making it the most socially integrated slave system in history.
- Employed in primary, secondary and tertiary (service) industries.
- Slaves were also employed in quarternary industries.
- The Roman concept of agency was difficult to conceptualize, so slaves were a workaround: slave owners were held responsible for the acts of an enslaved person, since they were extensions of their masters' legal personality.
- Eunuchs were also prized as a special class of enslaved person. 
- Sex work was also common, though Augustus banned it later.

Experiences of Enslavement — Violent Domination
- Sexual assault was a common experience for Roman slaves, even if they were not exploited in the sex trade.
- Many male masters freed and married their slaves.
- Slaves could be sexually abused with no consequences.
- Hunger was used to control the enslaved.
- Slaves who attempted to flee could be chained, or tattooed for easy recognition.
	- After this practice was forbidden by Constantine, they were given collars instead.
- Corporal abuse and punishment was common, sometimes even for the entertainment of the master.
- Slaves could also be killed or crucified.
- They were permanently outsiders to Roman civic and political society. 
- Treated as livestock, especially when sold at slave auctions and completely humiliated.
- Considered part of the familia, and called them words similar to those they called children.
	- Could amass wealth and start their own families, and buy their freedom, but they would still have obligations to their previous owner.

Exits from Slavery
- The most common exit was death, usually due to overwork and abuse, but sometimes due to suicide.
- Only legitimate means out of slavery: manumission.
- Often conferred citizen status after manumission. 
- Manumission was used as an incentive and mechanism of controlling enslaved persons.
