Facts:
- Appellant convicted for murder of victim. 
- Appellant visited victim's wife late at night. Victim came home and there was an altercation. Appellant stabbed victim in the chest, killing him. 
- Injury was enough to cause death in the ordinary course of nature, cause of death was haemorrhage and shock due to injuries. 

Arguments:
Appellant: argued that the act was in self-defense (did not hold up in court, since there was no evidence of a physical fight)
- Intention: said he had no intention ([[Y1 T3 Criminal Law I/CONCEPTS/Mens rea\|Mens rea]]) to kill the victim (did not hold up, with the Court contending that he had knowledge that it was likely to kill him)

Court's decision:
- Virsa Singh principles: (1) body injury is present, (2) injury is sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death, (3) the accused intended to inflict that particular injury (not accidental, unintentional or that some other kind of injury was intended)
- Accused convicted of murder.
