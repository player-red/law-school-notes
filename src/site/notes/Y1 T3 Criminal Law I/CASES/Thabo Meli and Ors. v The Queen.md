Facts:
The defendants took the victim to a hut, got him intoxicated and hit him. Believing that they had killed him with this strike, they rolled him down a hill to make it look like it was an accident. 
Medical analysis showed that it was the exposure that had killed the victim. 

Issue:
1. Whether the lack of guilty intent when they rolled him down the hill means they are not responsible for his murder.

Decision: 
- Conviction for murder upheld.
- All their acts were part of a preconceived plan to kill the victim, so which act killed the victim does not matter as much. 
	- Impossible to divide the single transaction that occurred.
