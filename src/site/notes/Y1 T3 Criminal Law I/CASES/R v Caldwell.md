Facts:
- Defendant set fire to the hotel where he worked. 
- He says he was so drunk that he did not realize he was endangering the lives of people. (voluntary intoxication)

Court's decision: 
- Intoxication can be considered when discussing crimes of intent. However, self-induced intoxication is not a defense and is reckless.
- "Basic intent" crimes: drunkenness can be ignored. However, drunkenness must be taken into consideration for "specific intent" crimes.
	- Simple arson -> basic intent
	- Aggravated arson -> specific intent
- Carrying out an act knowing there is some risk of damage: recklessness. 
	- Intoxication is a defence, but not in cases where it is a danger to life. Self-induced intoxication is an exception. 
- A man should be punished for what his state of mind actually was, not what one thinks it ought to have been. 
- Appeal dismissed.
