Facts: 
A police constable questioning the defendant asked him to park his vehicle. The defendant drove the vehicle onto the policeman's foot. After the constable shouted for him to get off his foot multiple times, the defendant removed the car from the police constable's foot. 
Assault proved on the grounds of keeping the vehicle on the foot knowingly, since it could not be determined whether the defendant had the intention to drive the car onto the constable's foot. 

Issues:
1. Whether the defendant committed assault?

Decision:
- An omission does not amount to assault: his omission to act was a continual act of battery. 
- When he decided not to cease his continuous act, after he  realized he had driven his car onto the foot of the officer, [[Y1 T3 Criminal Law I/CONCEPTS/Mens rea\|Mens rea]] was established.
