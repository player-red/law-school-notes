Facts:
Terrible rape of a woman sitting alone in the ladies compartment, after he pushed her out/she fell out of the train. Died after being kept in a supine position during rape. Other passengers wanted to intervene but were stopped by a middle-aged man.

Issues:
1. Whether the accused can be held guilty for murder?
2. Whether the fall from the train that ultimately led to the death of the victim can be attributed to the accused?

Arguments:
Appellant's arguments:
- He did not have the intention to murder her. 
- No clear evidence that he pushed her out of the train, resulting in her death. 

Respondent's arguments:
- Accused was a habitual offender who went into the ladies compartment on realizing she was alone. 
- 'Rarest of the rare doctrine' argued for.

Decision:
- Held guilty of rape but not murder.
- Accused could not be held liable for the second injury (from the train) because it could not be proved how/why she fell from the train/jumped.
	- Could have argued that even if she jumped from the train, it would have been due to the actions of the accused.
