Aka R v Warner

Facts:
- Defendant found in possession of a prohibited drug.
- Said he was ignorant to the contents of the parcel.

Decision:
- Convicted.
- The offence was an absolute one and did not require [[Y1 T3 Criminal Law I/CONCEPTS/Mens rea\|Mens rea]] for conviction. 
- However, a defendant could be acquitted if:
	- It could be shown that they genuinely believed the parcel to have innocent substances.
	- Had no reasonable opportunity of examining its contents.
