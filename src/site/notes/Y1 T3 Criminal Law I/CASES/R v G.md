Facts:
- Defendants, minor boys (11 and 12) went camping and set fire to some newspapers and threw them under a plastic dustbin without putting out the fire. 
- The dustbin caught fire and caused damages worth 1 million pounds. 
- Charged with arson (recklessness). 

Arguments of defendants at trial:
- Expected the fire to extinguish itself
- Did not expect it to spread, did not understand the risk. 

Issues:
1. Whether the defendants acted recklessly?
	- Did they fail to think about the risk or did they recognize it and do it anyway?
2. Whether the rick can be judged by what a reasonable person would think rather than considering the personal traits of the defendants (age/mental capacity)?

Decision:
- Appeal allowed and conviction quashed. 
- Recklessness requires actual foresight of the risk.
- Committing an actus reus inadvertently is not sufficient for conviction of a serious crime. 
	- Especially when defendant is limited by age, intellect, etc.
- Appropriate test is subjective.
