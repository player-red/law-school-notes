[[Y1 T3 Contracts II/CONCEPTS/Indemnity\|Indemnity]]
Facts:
- Plaintiff entered into an agreement with the BMC in 1934 for lease of a plot of land in Dadar for 999 years. 
- Plaintiff obtained possession. 
- Transferred benefit of lease to defendant to construct a building. 
- Defendant obtained materials from Keshavads Mohandas but was unable to pay him. 
- Hence, the plaintiff:
	- Jan 14 1937: mortgaged land for 5000 rs to pay KM
	- March 23 1937: mortgaged land again for 5000 more
- Due date: Jan 14, 1938.
- Jul 30 1939: Plaintiff wrote a letter asking the Defendant to be the lessee.
- Defendant failed to release Plaintiff from the mortgage. 

Arguments of the parties:
Plaintiff: there was an implied contract to indemnify (because the mortgages were made at request of the defendant), and the defendant should have obtained the plaintiff's release when he became the lessee.
Defendant: until the indemnified has suffered a loss, the indemnifier cannot be sued, and in this case the plaintiff has not suffered a loss. 

Decision:
- Indemnity arises because the plaintiff has become liable owing to something which he has done at the request of the defendant.
- Sec. 124 does not apply, and Sec. 125 is not exhaustive.
- English common law: no action can be maintained if no actual loss incurred. However, court argues that the value of an indemnity is lost if the indemnified cannot enforce the indemnity till an actual loss is suffered. 
- Defendant held liable (had to release plaintiff from mortgage and pay off the amount due to the mortgagee).
