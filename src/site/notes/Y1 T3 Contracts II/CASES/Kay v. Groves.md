A case involving a [[Y1 T3 Contracts II/CONCEPTS/Guarantee\|Guarantee]].

Facts:
- Groves (guarantor) agreed to be held answerable to Mr Kay (creditor) for Taylor (principal debtor). 
- For five sacks of flour to be delivered to Taylor within one month's time. 
- Kay delivered 5 sacks on the 19th of November, and then five on the 21st.
- Groves only paid for 1 1/2 because 3 1/2 sacks (out of the first 5) were bad quality.
- Kay contends that Groves should pay for each sack delivered (not exceeding 5 sacks).

Arguments:
- Defendant says he only liable for what has actually been furnished, ie 1 1/2 sacks. 
- Plaintiff says liability began from 19th November, liable for flour sent not exceeding five sacks. 

Decision:
- Jury's decision was that the defendant was only liable for 1 1/2 sacks, and the guarantee did not continue beyond the first five sacks. Judges all agreed.
