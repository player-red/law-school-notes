- The benefit each party derives from the performance of the contract's promises.
- A promised performance or any conduct under Section 2(d), ICA. 
- Must have some [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Value\|Value]] in the eyes of the law.
- It need not be adequate.
	- When [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Consent\|Consent]] is freely given, the agreement is not void if the consideration is merely inadequate.
	- Inadequacy of the consideration may be taken into account by the Court when determining whether consent was freely given.
- Any contract that lacks consideration is void. Section 25 (1) of the [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/READINGS/Indian Contract Act, 1872\|Indian Contract Act, 1872]] is an exception which states that as long as there is formality (registered under law), consideration is not required. 
  Another exception: when someone has already voluntarily done something for the promisor or something which the promisor was legally compellable to do. 
- Past consideration is not good consideration. 
- Interpreting Section 25(2) of the ICA: [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Nexus\|Nexus]] still required despite past consideration. 
- Section 57: reciprocal promises.
	- Mutual promises are legal = valid contract.
	- Mutual promises are illegal = no valid contract.

- Section 23: lawful consideration, unless
	- Forbidden by law or would defeat the provisions of any law
	- Fraudulent
	- Injury to a person or property of another
	- Court regards it as immoral or opposed to [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Public policy\|Public policy]].
		- Ex dolo malo non oritur actio. 
		- Nature of prohibition: statutory, common law, RBI circular (if disclosed to public).

Illegality of consideration
- Illegality must be sufficiently connected with the agreement.
	- [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Object\|Object]] of the contract.
- Illegal performance is not included, as long as illegality of the conduct is not what both parties intended at the time of formation of the contract. 
- Limitations:
	- Possibility of illegality is not enough to prove illegal. 
	- When the motive is different from object.
