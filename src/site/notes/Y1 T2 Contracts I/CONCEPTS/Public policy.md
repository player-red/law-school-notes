Treated alongside morality: if any of these two is violated, [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Consideration\|Consideration]] is considered illegal. 

Violations of public policy/morality that would void an agreement:
- Interfering with the administration of justice: an agreement to withdraw criminal proceedings.
- Bribery and other crimes related to public administration. 
- Marriage and family life: ??
- Unconscionable contracts: SC tried to create in the Brojo Nath case, only applicable to the government. 
	- Entities recognised as State under [[ARCHIVE/Y1/Y1 T1/Y1 T1 Legal Methods/CONSTITUTION/Article 12\|Article 12]]. 
	- Interpretation of [[ARCHIVE/Y1/Y1 T1/Y1 T1 Legal Methods/CONSTITUTION/Article 14\|Article 14]]: prohibition of arbitrary action by the State. 
	- Do not apply to private actors (individuals and companies.

Though violative of fundamental rights, does not apply in private contracts:
- Constitutional policy: any private agreement that violates Constitutional principles ==should== be void (ie [[ARCHIVE/Y1/Y1 T1/Y1 T1 Legal Methods/CASE ANALYSIS/LM12.02 Zoroastrian Co-Operative Housing Society Ltd. v District Registrar, Cooperative Societies\|LM12.02 Zoroastrian Co-Operative Housing Society Ltd. v District Registrar, Cooperative Societies]]), however is not.
