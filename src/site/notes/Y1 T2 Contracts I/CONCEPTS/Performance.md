Parties to a contract must perform or offer to perform their respective promises unless it is dispensed with or excused under the [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/READINGS/Indian Contract Act, 1872\|Indian Contract Act, 1872]].
- Is not a unilateral act.
- Representatives of promisors bound if promisor dies before performance, unless contract intends otherwise.
	- *Eg: A promises to paint a picture for B by a certain day, at a certain price. A dies before the day. Is A excused?*
	  Yes, A would be excused since it would be assumed that it was a personal performance, and that B intending to employ A's skill.
Excuses that dispense performance:
- Section 38: if someone is offering to perform, and if that offer is refused. In this case, the promisor would not be bound by any promise, and does not lose any rights under the contract.
- Section 39: when a party has refused to perform, or disabled themselves from performing, the promisee may put an end to the contract, unless the promisee  signifies intent for continuance.
- Section 40: Who has to perform - the promisor or any other competent third party employed by the promisor.
- Section 41: when performance by a third party is accepted by the promisee, they have no right to sue on this performance.

Time and place for performance
- Section 46: must be completed in reasonable time when no time for performance is specified + when no application made (demanding performance).
- Section 47: when time and place are specified but no application has to be made — the day of performance is given, and when the promisor has agreed to do it without application, the performance must be done at any time during business hours at the specified place.
- Section 48: performance on certain day when application is required.
- Section 49: when no place is specified, and no application is to be made — duty of the promisor to apply to the promisee to appoint a specific place.
- Section 50: performance in manner or at time sanctioned by promisee.
