Sec. 31 - 36, [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/READINGS/Indian Contract Act, 1872\|Indian Contract Act, 1872]].
Definition (Sec 31): a contract to do or not to do something if some event does or does not happen (collateral to the contract).
Example: insurance contracts.
Conditions:
- No liability till the event occurs.
- The event must be collateral to the contract, and not the performance of the contract. 
- The event should be out of control of both parties. 
- Before the event occurs, both parties may withdraw.
- Neither party must do anything to prevent the occurrence of the event. 

- Void when the event becomes impossible. 
- Void if the event becomes impossible/has not occurred within a fixed period of time (if specified).
