Sec. 27, [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/READINGS/Indian Contract Act, 1872\|Indian Contract Act, 1872]]. 
Any agreement which involves restraint of trade as [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Consideration\|Consideration]] is void, unless a business is sold, in which case restraints must be limited, local, and appear reasonable to Courts. Usually called negative covenants.

- Competing policies: freedom of contract v protection of a person's fundamental right to earn a living. 
- [[ARCHIVE/Y1/Y1 T1/Y1 T1 Legal Methods/CONSTITUTION/Article 19\|Article 19]] (1) (a) freedom of speech and expression, (g) freedom of profession, occupation, trade, business.
- Sec. 27 does not have much scope for reading reasonableness.
- Court makes distinctions between Absolute and Partial restraints.
- Restraint during the term of the contract is used as a proxy for reasonableness, generally allowed.
	- Not allowed if unnecessarily harsh, unreasonable or one-sided.
	- Restraint operating after termination of contract is void.
	- Exception: prohibition from revealing confidential information (trade secrets, proprietary information) does not restrict a person from gaining employment.
