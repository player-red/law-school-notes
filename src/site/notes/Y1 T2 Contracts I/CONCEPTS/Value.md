- Famously defined in Curie v Misa, as the interest or benefit accruing to one party or some detriment or loss undertaken by another. 
- Must be real and not illusory. 
- It is not adequacy of consideration. 
- [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Consideration\|Consideration]] must have some “value” in the eyes of the law. 
- Despite [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Nexus\|Nexus]] existing along with the desire of the parties, if the consideration has no value in the eyes of the law, it is invalid.
