Putting an end to the contract without performing the promises in it. 
- It may be express or implied from the conduct of the parties.
- Rescinded on terms agreed upon by the parties.
- Either termination or avoidance.
Sec. 62, [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/READINGS/Indian Contract Act, 1872\|Indian Contract Act, 1872]]: mutual rescission, [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Novation\|Novation]] or [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Alteration\|Alteration]] - the original contract need not be performed in these 3 cases.
Sec. 63: the promisee may dispense with or remit the performance of a promise.
Sec. 64: rescission of a voidable contract (option of one party)

Sec. 65: when a contract is shown to be void or becomes void, the person who gained an advantage from it must compensate the person who gave it.
Sec. 66: same rules as communication or revocation of a proposal. 
Sec. 67: if the promisee does not offer reasonable facilities to the promisor in order to perform, the promisor is excused for non-performance.
