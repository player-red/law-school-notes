Sec. 17 of the [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/READINGS/Indian Contract Act, 1872\|Indian Contract Act, 1872]].
An Act intended to deceive another party to enter into a contract, including:
- Suggestion of a fact that is not true, by someone who does not believe it is true.
- Active concealment of a fact by someone having knowledge or belief of it
- A promise made without any intention of performing it
- Any other act fitted to deceive
- Any act or omission the law declares to be fraudulent.

Exception: Silence that may affect the willingness of a person to enter into a contract does not amount to fraud unless a duty exists to speak, or unless silence is speech. 

Section 19 — Exception
If consent caused by misrepresentation or silence, fraudulent within sec. 17, contract is not voidable if the party whose consent was so caused could discover the truth through ordinary diligence.
