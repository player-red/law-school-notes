Facts:
ONGC entered into a contract with Burn Standard for the construction of an oil rig, who then further subcontracted the construction to McDermont (MII). Work was to be started on 1st Jan 1986 and completed by 1st Jan 1988. 
Dispute between MII and Burn Standard, in which the former begins arbitration on 10th April 1989. Arbitrator decides in favour of MII, and awards damages. ONGC challenges this award as being opposed to [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Public policy\|Public policy]].
Terms of the contract: no extensions permitted unless authorized in writing, no portion of works must extend beyond monsoon 1986 monsoon. Also provided for damages in case of delay in execution. 

Judgement:
- Time was not of the essence to the contract, hence the contract was not voidable under Sec 55 of the [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/READINGS/Indian Contract Act, 1872\|Indian Contract Act, 1872]]. 
- In construction contracts, it is generally presumed that time is not of the essence.
