Facts:
Respondent boarded flight at 5:45 am, scheduled to fly at 6:15 am. Buys a sandwich for Rs 100. 11:15 am, an announcement is made that the flight is cancelled, and three choices were given: (a) refund, (b) credit for future travel, (c) rebooking at no cost. Chose rebooking, delayed due to weather conditions and not provided with food on time. After reaching Hyderabad, the respondent filed a complaint in the Permanent Lok Adalat for Public Utility Services, demanding compensation for physical discomfort, mental agony and inconvenience.
Appellant stated that the Lok Adalat at Hyderabad did not have jurisdiction, only courts at Delhi did.
Standard terms of contract: all disputes subject to the jurisdiction of courts of Delhi only.

Held: 
Standard terms held invalid (exclusive jurisdiction clause.)
Lok Adalats are conciliatory bodies, not adjudicatory, which was the only type of body mentioned in the contract.
