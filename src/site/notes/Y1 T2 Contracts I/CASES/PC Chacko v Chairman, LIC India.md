Supreme Court of India

Facts: 
Repudiation of insurance policy based on misstatement of facts, since it was material to the contract of insurance. Insured had undergone an adenoma thyroid operation but denied any such surgery while answering questions in the application form. 

Issue:
Whether the suppression of the fact (surgery) was a material fact.

Decision:
Application of Section 45, Insurance Act, 1938. 
(a) statement must be material or suppress facts which were material to disclose
(b) suppression must be fraudulently made
(c) policy holder must have known at the time of making the statement that it was false or suppressing facts.
[[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Uberrimae fidei\|Uberrimae fidei]] also used.
Held that the contract could be repudiated by the LIC, since the fact of the surgery was material to the contract.
