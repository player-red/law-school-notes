Facts: 
An agreement with an arbitration clause which proposed that the decision of the arbitrator was final and binding between the parties, and that they relinquished any right to appeal or object to the decision.
[[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Severability\|Severability]] clause: 
If one part of the agreement was invalid, illegal or unenforceable (as decided by a court), the remainder would still be enforceable, by treating the invalid parts as if they were never included.

Judgement:
Held that partial invalidity would not make the entire contract invalid or unenforceable. [[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Blue Pencil Doctrine\|Blue Pencil Doctrine]] used, petition upheld.
