Facts:
Defendants agreed to let the plaintiffs take a music hall on certain days. If they did not allow the plaintiffs use the Hall, they would have to repay all expenses incurred by the plaintiffs. Did not have any clause addressing destruction of the Hall, but it was a general trade practice that the contract would be rescinded in such a case. 
The Hall was destroyed by a fire, and the plaintiffs sued for damages.

Decision:
[[Y1 T2 Contracts I/CONCEPTS/Performance\|Performance]] of the contract became impossible due to unforeseen events. Hence, both parties are excused.
- Set up the doctrine of Frustration/Impossibility
